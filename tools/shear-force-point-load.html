DESIGN COMPLIANCE & ANALYSIS GUIDE

Shear Principles

For a point load, the shear force remains constant at $+R_1$ until the load point, where it drops instantly to $-R_2$.

Moment Principles

The moment diagram is linear (triangular). Maximum bending moment always occurs directly under the concentrated load.

Critical Points

Zero Shear point corresponds to the point of Maximum Bending Moment ($M_{max}$).

Engineering Tip: Always check for local web buckling or crushing at the point of application for large concentrated loads.
